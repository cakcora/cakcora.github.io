Biography

Dr. Cuneyt Akcora is an Associate Professor at the University of Central Florida with appointments in the Department of Finance in the College of Business and the Department of Computer Science in the College of Engineering and Computer Science. He is a faculty member of the UCF AI Institute and the FinTech Initiative, where he teaches and conducts research in financial AI, graph machine learning, and blockchain data analytics.

He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Insubria, Italy, and his M.S. in Computer Science and Engineering from the State University of New York at Buffalo. Before joining UCF, he was a Postdoctoral Fellow in Computer Science and Statistics at the University of Texas at Dallas and an Assistant Professor of Computer Science and Statistics at the University of Manitoba. He is a former Fulbright Scholar.

His research develops machine learning and topological methods for temporal graphs, financial networks, blockchain systems, and trustworthy artificial intelligence. His recent work has appeared at ICLR, NeurIPS, KDD, VLDB, ICDM, SDM, IJCAI, ICDE, and IEEE Transactions. He is the author of the course book Blockchain: Fundamentals, Data Structures and Algorithms for Data Science, published by Cambridge University Press.

Dr. Akcora has led and co-led research projects supported by NSF, NSERC, Research Manitoba, Mitacs, UCF, and industry partners. His recent publications include TopoFormer and ATEX-CF at ICLR 2026, MiNT and TopER at NeurIPS 2025, GOttack at ICLR 2025, and Chainlet Orbits at KDD 2025.
